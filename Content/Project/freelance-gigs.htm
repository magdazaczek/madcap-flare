Freelance gigs

In today's new world of work, we can observe that freelance gigs become more popular. There's a significant growth in number of people who are self-employed, or work as freelancers and collaborate with many partners or platforms. Freelance gigs are a good way to get some extra money, but is it really the best possible path as many young people believe?

Of course, every solution has its advantages and disadvantages, but before you consider becoming a freelancer, let's take a look at advantages and disadvantages below.

Advantages

The main advantages of being a freelancer are:

  • Freedom - you decide how you work, what clients you want to collaborate with.

  • Flexibility - you may set your own working rules, nobody controls your working hours or place from where you work.

  • Greater independence - it's really important for people who aren't team players, and don't want to adjust to any corporate world.

  • Pay - many experienced freelancers make a lot of money, mainly thanks to their experience and word-of-mouth marketing.

Disadvantages

Being a freelancer is always a bed of roses. Especially as a aspiring freelancer, you may find it really difficult to enter that world. Check the list of disadvantages below to see if there's anything you wouldn't accept.

  • No stability - you never know how many projects or clients you'll get.

  • No benefits - if you've always dreamed of having fancy benefits, medical insurance, or budget for training courses, you'll have to cover all these costs on your own.

  • Isolation - people who like working with others may have a problem with being alone all the time at work.

  • More stress - if your position in the market in now stable, you may face many challenges on your way. It can be difficult to compete with other freelancers, especially with those from other countries who will offer the same services but two times cheaper than you.

Platforms for freelancers

Many freelancers started their career by doing side gigs after their regular working hours. That's a safe step to take. Consider offering your services on the following platforms to see what the situation in the market looks like before you decide to leave your current job.

  2. Fiverr
