Hybrid working model

Hybrid working models seems to be a compromise for those who want to work remote, but also visit offices from time to time.

Companies that decide for that working model usually define how frequent you need to come to the office. Many of them set targets weekly or monthly. There are also employers who decide to that everybody will come to the office on a specific day.

Best practices for a successful hybrid working model

The table presents best practices and recommendations for companies that are switching to the hybrid working model.

Area to think about Description
Policies Define clear policies for your employees. People have to know what the rules are. Are they free to decide when they want to come to the office? Should they come to the office at a specified frequency?
Office It's important to verify if everybody will have their own desk in the office. If it's not possible, consider creating a system for making reservations to avoid an uncomfortable situation when someone comes to the office and doesn't have a place to sit.
Employees If your company has been operating only remotely for the last few years and people live in different places, it might be a problem to have them all at the office. Verify that and gather feedback from your employees on that topic.
