4-day-week

The discussion about a 4-day work week has become more and more popular recently. There are many companies, not only in Europe, but around the world that started trialling this approach.

What does a 4-day work week mean in reality?

The initiative intends to:

  • Boost productivity while reducing working hours.

  • Retain salaries at the same level.

  • Increase happiness among staff.

Companies that tested 4-day work week

The table presents a list of companies that decided to test a 4-day-work week.

Company name Country Key findings
Exos USA Happier and more productive employees.
Microsoft Japan Japan Increased productivity by 40%, reduced energy consumption by 23.1%, and improved employee satisfaction.
Atom Bank UK No negative impact on productivity, and employees reported feeling less stressed and more engaged.
Perdoo USA Increased productivity, improved employee morale, and reduced employee turnover.
Doist Global Increased productivity, improved employee satisfaction, and better work-life balance.
